The product

This is AEGIS

The AEGIS smart helmet — rear-facing camera, on-device detection, and directional LED + vibration alerts built into the shell.

The problem

Riders can't reliably see what's behind them

+13%
rise in US bicyclist deaths, 2021 → 2022 (976 → 1,105)
48.3%
of all road fatalities in India (2024) were two-wheelers

Mirrors leave large blind spots, and turning to check traffic costs balance and reaction time. Existing blind-spot add-ons mostly offer raw proximity alerts, without telling a genuinely closing threat apart from harmless passing traffic — and without working offline.

How it works

Six steps, entirely on the helmet

No cloud round-trip sits between a threat and the rider's warning — detection, scoring, and alerting all happen locally, in real time.

1
Capture
Rear camera streams footage of the road behind the rider.
2
Detect
Tracks each vehicle, cyclist, or pedestrian frame-to-frame.
3
Score
Measures proximity and how fast each object is closing in.
4
Classify
Flags the single genuine threat, filtering out passing traffic.
5
Alert
Light + vibration fire instantly, no network round-trip.
6
Archive
Alert + clip sync here whenever a connection is available.
